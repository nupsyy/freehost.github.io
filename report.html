CopperStealer Malware Crew Resurfaces with New Rootkit and Phishing Kit Modules

In March and April 2023, the threat actors responsible for the CopperStealer malware reemerged with two new campaigns aimed at delivering two novel payloads known as CopperStealth and CopperPhish. Under the name "Water Orthrus," Trend Micro is keeping track of the group that is motivated by money.

Another campaign known as Scranos, which Bitdefender detailed in 2019, is also thought to be backed by the adversary. "Security researchers Jaromir Horejsi and Joseph C Chen stated in a technical report that CopperStealth's infection chain involves dropping and loading a rootkit, which then injects its payload into explorer.exe and another system process."

"These payloads are liable for downloading and running extra assignments. The rootkit likewise impedes admittance to blocklisted library keys and keeps certain executables and drivers from running." CopperStealth also has a task module that gives it the ability to call a remote server and retrieve the command that will be executed on the infected machine. This gives the malware the ability to drop additional payloads.

The CopperPhish campaign, which was discovered all over the world in April 2023, uses a similar method to spread malware via PPI networks that are hidden behind free, anonymous file-sharing websites..
